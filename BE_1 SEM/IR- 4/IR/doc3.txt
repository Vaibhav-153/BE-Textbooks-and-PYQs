Search engines rely on information retrieval techniques to provide relevant results to users.
They use inverted indexes to map query terms to corresponding documents.
The preprocessing steps include tokenization, stopword removal, and stemming.
Natural language processing helps in understanding the intent of the user’s query.
Query processing involves analyzing the query and matching it with indexed documents.
Ranking algorithms ensure that the most relevant results appear at the top.
Evaluation metrics such as precision, recall, and F-measure are used to test system accuracy.
Search engines handle millions of queries daily with the help of distributed indexing.
Machine learning models improve personalization and ranking of search results.
Modern IR systems also incorporate big data and NLP for large-scale document retrieval.
Information retrieval techniques are not limited to text — they extend to images, audio, and video.
Optimized indexing and query processing reduce response time and improve user satisfaction.
Efficient search systems save time and improve decision-making for users.
