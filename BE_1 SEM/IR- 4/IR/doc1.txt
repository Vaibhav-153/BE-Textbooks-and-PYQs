Information retrieval is the process of obtaining relevant information from large collections of data.
It plays a vital role in modern technology such as search engines, digital libraries, and online databases.
Users provide queries to find the information they need efficiently.
The main objective of information retrieval is to locate relevant documents that match user needs.
Indexing helps improve search performance by organizing documents systematically.
Tokenization converts text into individual words for analysis.
Stop word removal eliminates unnecessary words like "the", "and", and "is".
Stemming reduces words to their root form to improve retrieval accuracy.
Relevance ranking ensures that the most useful documents appear first in search results.
Evaluation metrics like precision, recall, and F1-score are used to measure system performance.
Modern IR systems often integrate machine learning and natural language processing techniques.
Big data has increased both the opportunities and challenges for information retrieval systems.
Effective IR systems enhance user satisfaction and save time by providing accurate results.
